About Nuclear Energy
Currently in the United States, nuclear power only provides 9% of our energy, lagging behind our largest sources of energy, oil and natural gas, which together produce 70% of our energy. And with many reactors slated for retirement, US will actually have less nuclear power in 2050 than now in 2020.
Many environmentalists oppose nuclear power in favor of wind and solar, but these power sources are inconsistent. The sun doesn't always shine, nor does the wind always blow, resulting in far less power produced than nuclear energy or natural gas. And Nuclear power is actually comparatively safer to birds and mammals than wind turbines or solar panels.
Meltdowns like those in Fukushima, Chernobyl, and Three Mile Island have certainly hurt nuclear power. New reactors however, are far safer than those built half a century ago. They use Thorium instead of Uranium, which not only produces far less waste, but also allows for far more ease in stopping reactions, as it far more fissile, meaning the isotope is easier to split, creating safer energy.
